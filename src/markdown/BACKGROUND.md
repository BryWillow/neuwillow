
The Weeping Willow is my favorite tree. <br />
I find them fascinating as often times they can grow so tall, with branches so long,<br /> 
they touch the ground and form a canopy we can't see inside. When you part the branches,<br />
walk inside, you can often disappear in the silence. You become aware of all its intricacies,<br />
overlapping paths of branches, networks big and small, all of which are a product 
of its unique pattern of growth.<br /><br />

To me this tree is mysterious, and I've wondered what what makes it thrive, and what it hides. <br />
However, the lifespan of these trees can be relatiely short, and they are susceptible to disease. <br />
That said, "neu" is simply an abbreviation of neuron, and "willow", my favorite tree. <br />
I, personally, felt a lot of parallels between these two things and the human brain.<br />
Hence the name, "neuwillow".<br />
